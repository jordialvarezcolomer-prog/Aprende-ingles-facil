## Tema 1: Artículo Académico

### Ejercicio 1: Lee el artículo sobre fenomenología

**Artículo:**
```
PHENOMENOLOGICAL APPROACHES TO CONSCIOUSNESS: A REASSESSMENT
The phenomenological tradition, stemming from Husserl's work, emphasizes 
the first-person perspective in understanding consciousness. Contemporary 
neuroscience, however, privileges third-person empirical observation. This 
apparent dichotomy has generated extensive philosophical debate. Some 
scholars advocate for a reconciliation of these approaches, arguing that 
subjective experience and objective neural correlates are complementary 
rather than contradictory. This paper examines the theoretical implications 
of integrating phenomenological and scientific frameworks.
```

**Análisis Crítico:**
1. ¿Cuál es la tensión principal identificada?
2. ¿Qué postura proponen algunos estudiosos?
3. ¿Es el autor a favor o en contra de la integración?

**Respuestas:** 1. First-person vs. third-person perspectives | 2. Reconciliation of approaches | 3. (Implicado como favorable)

## Tema 2: Crítica Literaria Avanzada

### Ejercicio 1: Lee la crítica de un trabajo literario

**Crítica:**
```
POSTMODERN DECONSTRUCTION IN CONTEMPORARY FICTION
The author's innovative narrative technique subverts conventional 
storytelling paradigms through metanarrative interventions and temporal 
fragmentation. The interplay of intertextual references creates a 
palimpsest that challenges reader expectations. While this postmodern 
approach demonstrates considerable literary sophistication, it risks 
alienating readers through excessive formal complexity. Nevertheless, 
the work constitutes a significant contribution to contemporary literature.
```

**Preguntas:**
1. Identifica dos técnicas narrativas utilizadas.
2. ¿Cuál es la crítica implícita al enfoque?
3. ¿Cuál es la evaluación final?

**Respuestas:** 1. Metanarrative and temporal fragmentation | 2. Excessive complexity | 3. Significant contribution

## Tema 3: Ensayo Filosófico

### Ejercicio 1: Lee el ensayo sobre ética

**Ensayo:**
```
THE PHENOMENOLOGY OF MORAL OBLIGATION
Moral obligation presents a paradox: we experience it as compelling yet 
justify it through rational frameworks. Kantian deontology privileges 
duty above consequence, while utilitarianism prioritizes outcomes. Neither 
framework adequately captures the phenomenological reality of moral 
experience—the felt urgency of ethical imperatives. This essay proposes 
that a phenomenological approach, examining how moral obligations manifest 
in lived experience, provides crucial insights that purely rationalist 
ethics overlook. The integration of first-person phenomenological analysis 
with ethical theory offers a richer understanding of moral phenomena.
```

**Análisis:**
1. ¿Cuál es la limitación de los enfoques tradicionales?
2. ¿Qué solución propone el autor?

**Respuestas:** 1. They don't capture lived moral experience | 2. Integration of phenomenological analysis with ethical theory

## Tema 4: Reporte de Investigación Avanzado

### Ejercicio 1: Lee el reporte científico

**Reporte:**
```
NEUROPLASTICITY AND COGNITIVE REHABILITATION: 
A META-ANALYSIS OF LONGITUDINAL STUDIES
This meta-analysis synthesizes findings from 47 longitudinal studies 
examining neuroplasticity's role in cognitive recovery post-stroke. 
Results indicate that intensive, task-specific training correlates with 
significant functional recovery (Cohen's d = 0.89, p < .001). 
Critically, neuroimaging data demonstrates that recovery correlates with 
reorganization of neural networks, not restoration of damaged tissue. 
Implications suggest that rehabilitation protocols should emphasize 
intensive, goal-directed training to maximize neuroplastic potential.
```

**Análisis Metodológico:**
1. ¿Cuántos estudios se incluyeron en el meta-análisis?
2. ¿Cuál es el tamaño del efecto reportado?
3. ¿Cuál es el descubrimiento clave sobre el mecanismo?

**Respuestas:** 1. 47 | 2. Cohen's d = 0.89 | 3. Neural network reorganization, not tissue restoration

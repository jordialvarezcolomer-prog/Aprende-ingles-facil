## Tema 1: Artículo Académico Densamente Teórico

### Ejercicio 1: Lee el artículo sobre epistemología

**Artículo:**
```
EPISTEMOLOGICAL FRAMEWORKS IN LATE MODERNITY: 
RECONCILING EMPIRICISM AND CONSTRUCTIVISM

The contemporary epistemological landscape reflects an increasingly 
untenable dichotomy between empiricist and constructivist paradigms. 
Both frameworks possess considerable theoretical merit, yet neither 
adequately captures the nuances of knowledge production in late modernity. 
This article proposes a synthetic approach—critical realism—that 
preserves empiricism's commitment to ontological independence while 
acknowledging constructivism's insights into the theory-laden nature 
of observation. Through an examination of scientific practice, we 
demonstrate that knowledge production constitutes neither a passive 
reflection of reality nor a solipsistic construction, but rather an 
intersubjective negotiation within socially embedded practices of inquiry.
```

**Análisis Profundo:**
1. Identifica la tesis principal y sus premisas fundamentales.
2. ¿Cómo se distingue el realismo crítico de los dos paradigmas anteriores?
3. ¿Cuáles son las implicaciones ontológicas del argumento?

**Respuestas:** 1. Synthesis needed between empiricism and constructivism; premises: both have merit but limitations | 2. Preserves both perspectives while transcending dichotomy | 3. Reality exists independently but is known through socially embedded practices

## Tema 2: Crítica Literaria Postestructuralista

### Ejercicio 1: Lee el análisis crítico avanzado

**Crítica:**
```
TEXTUAL INDETERMINACY AND THE PROBLEMATICS OF AUTHORIAL INTENTION
Derrida's deconstructive methodology fundamentally challenges the 
valorization of authorial intention in hermeneutics. The trace structure 
of the sign—its perpetual deferral of meaning—renders determinacy a 
chimera that critical practice inadvertently privileges. This analysis 
examines how poststructuralist frameworks illuminate the aporias within 
textual interpretation, foregrounding the irremediable alterity of the 
text and the impossibility of suturing meaning through recourse to 
authorial consciousness. The implications extend beyond literary theory, 
gesturing toward broader epistemological and political dimensions.
```

**Análisis Crítico Reflexivo:**
1. ¿Cuál es la crítica implícita a la hermenéutica tradicional?
2. Define "aporias" en este contexto y explica su relevancia.
3. ¿Cuáles serían las implicaciones políticas sugeridas?

**Respuestas:** 1. It assumes determinable authorial intention; misrecognizes meaning as determinate | 2. Irresolvable contradictions in interpretation; suggest limits of understanding | 3. (Abierto a interpretación: cuestiona poder, significado, autoridad)

## Tema 3: Monografía Académica (Extracto)

### Ejercicio 1: Lee el fragmento de investigación

**Fragmento:**
```
GENEALOGIES OF BIOPOWER: FOUCAULDIAN TRAJECTORIES IN CONTEMPORARY 
GOVERNANCE
Following Foucault's analytics, contemporary governance mechanisms exceed 
juridical prohibition, operating instead through the productive 
disciplining of life itself. The clinic, the school, and the prison 
constitute exemplary sites where biopower exercised through panoptic 
surveillance subtly orchestrates comportment and subjectivity. Yet 
contemporary configurations manifest increasing sophistication: genomic 
technologies, algorithmic categorization, and affective modulation 
constitute emergent modalities of power. These mechanisms operate not 
solely through repression but through the incitement and management of 
life. The genealogy traced here reveals how governmentality has evolved 
from primarily juridical to biopolitical frameworks.
```

**Síntesis y Crítica:**
1. Sintetiza el concepto de biopower según el autor.
2. ¿Cómo evolucionan las formas de control/poder según el argumento?
3. ¿Cuál es la lacuna potencial en este análisis?

**Respuestas:** 1. Productive disciplining of life through surveillance; not merely prohibitive | 2. From juridical to biopolitical; increasing sophistication with technology | 3. (Variable: posible insuficiencia en agentividad, resistencia)

## Tema 4: Tratado Filosófico (Extracto Denso)

### Ejercicio 1: Comprensión de texto altamente abstracto

**Texto:**
```
ONTOLOGICAL DIFFERENCE AND TEMPORALITY: 
HEIDEGGER'S PHENOMENOLOGICAL INVESTIGATION

The fundamental ontological difference—that between Being and beings—
structures all philosophical inquiry yet remains perpetually eclipsed by 
metaphysics' obsessive focus on entities. Heidegger's innovation resides 
in his demonstration that Being itself possesses temporal character; 
temporality is not merely an attribute of particular beings but the 
horizon of Being's intelligibility. This reconception destabilizes 
classical metaphysics' edifice, revealing how substantialist ontologies 
obscure Being's processual nature. The implications suggest that any 
adequate ontology must remain attuned to temporality's constitutive role 
in the manifestation of presence.
```

**Interpretación Filosófica:**
1. Explica la distinción entre Being y beings y su relevancia.
2. ¿Por qué temporalidad es crucial para la reinterpretación de Being?
3. ¿Qué critica implícitamente Heidegger en la metafísica tradicional?

**Respuestas:** 1. Being is the underlying condition; metaphysics focuses erroneously on beings | 2. Being manifests temporally; is not static/substance | 3. Substantialism obscures processual nature; static ontologies miss Being's dynamic character
